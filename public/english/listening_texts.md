## TEXT 1 - A1 Level (Monologue) - ~120 words
### Topic: My Family and Home

Hello, my name is Anna. I am twenty-three years old. I live in Almaty with my family. My family is not very big. There are four people in my family: my mother, my father, my younger brother, and me.

My mother is a teacher. She works at a school near our house. My father is an engineer. He works at a big company in the city center. My brother is still a student. He is seventeen years old and he goes to high school.

We live in a nice apartment. It has three bedrooms, a living room, and a kitchen. I like our home very much. On weekends, we often cook dinner together and watch movies.

## TEXT 2 - A1 Level (Dialogue) - ~130 words
### Topic: At the University

**Student A:** Hi! Are you new here?

**Student B:** Yes, this is my first day at the university. My name is Marat.

**Student A:** Nice to meet you, Marat. I'm Aisha. What are you studying?

**Student B:** I'm studying Computer Science. What about you?

**Student A:** I study International Relations. It's very interesting. Do you know where the library is?

**Student B:** No, I don't. I'm still learning where everything is.

**Student A:** It's next to the main building. I can show you after class.

**Student B:** That would be great, thank you. What time does your class finish?

**Student A:** At two o'clock. We can go to the cafeteria first and then to the library.

**Student B:** Perfect! See you then.

## TEXT 3 - A2 Level (Monologue) - ~150 words
### Topic: Healthy Lifestyle

Good morning, everyone. Today I want to talk about healthy living. Many people ask me how to stay healthy.

First, eating well is very important. You should eat more vegetables and fruits. Try to avoid fast food and too much sugar. Drinking enough water is also essential. Experts recommend drinking eight glasses of water every day.

Second, exercise regularly. You don't need to go to the gym every day. Walking for thirty minutes is enough. Some people prefer swimming or cycling. The important thing is to move your body.

Third, sleep is crucial for your health. Adults need seven to eight hours of sleep every night. Try to go to bed at the same time every day.

Finally, don't forget about mental health. Spend time with friends and family. Do activities that make you happy. A healthy mind is just as important as a healthy body.

## TEXT 4 - A2 Level (Interview) - ~160 words
### Topic: Work and Career

**Interviewer:** Welcome to our show. Today we have Dr. Kanat Omarov, a successful scientist. Dr. Omarov, can you tell us about your job?

**Dr. Omarov:** Of course. I work as a researcher at the National Science Center. My team studies renewable energy.

**Interviewer:** That sounds fascinating. How did you become a scientist?

**Dr. Omarov:** I was always curious about how things work. In university, I studied physics. After graduation, I got my PhD and started working at the research center.

**Interviewer:** What advice would you give to young people?

**Dr. Omarov:** Work hard and never stop learning. Science changes quickly, so you must always read new research. Also, don't be afraid to ask questions. Curiosity is the most important quality for a scientist.

**Interviewer:** Thank you for your time, Dr. Omarov.

**Dr. Omarov:** My pleasure. Thank you for having me.

## TEXT 5 - B1 Level (Monologue) - ~180 words
### Topic: Travel and Tourism in Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan is becoming an increasingly popular tourist destination. The country offers a unique combination of stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage.

One of the most famous attractions is Almaty, the former capital city. Visitors can explore the beautiful Medeu skating rink, the highest in the world, and take a cable car up to Shymbulak ski resort. The city has excellent restaurants, museums, and shopping centers.

Another must-see destination is Astana, now called Nursultan, the modern capital. The city features impressive architecture, including the Baiterek Tower, which has become a symbol of the country.

For nature lovers, the Charyn Canyon is often compared to the Grand Canyon in the United States. The dramatic rock formations and colorful cliffs attract thousands of tourists each year.

Kazakhstan is also famous for its hospitality. Kazakh people are known for being welcoming and generous to guests. Traditional cuisine, especially dishes like beshbarmak and kumys, offers visitors a taste of authentic Central Asian culture.

## TEXT 6 - B1 Level (Dialogue) - ~180 words
### Topic: Environmental Problems

**Professor:** Good afternoon, class. Today we'll discuss environmental challenges.

**Student:** Professor, what's the biggest environmental problem today?

**Professor:** That's a complex question. Climate change is perhaps the most serious issue. Global temperatures are rising, causing ice caps to melt and sea levels to rise.

**Student:** What causes climate change?

**Professor:** Mainly greenhouse gas emissions from burning fossil fuels—coal, oil, and natural gas. Transportation, factories, and power plants all contribute to the problem.

**Student:** Can we stop it?

**Professor:** We can slow it down if we act now. Many countries are investing in renewable energy sources like solar and wind power. Electric cars are becoming more common. Some nations have promised to become carbon neutral by 2050.

**Student:** What can ordinary people do?

**Professor:** Every action counts. Use public transport, reduce plastic consumption, recycle, and save energy at home. Small changes by millions of people can make a significant difference. Education is also crucial—the more people understand the problem, the more they'll want to help solve it.

## TEXT 7 - B1 Level (Interview) - ~190 words  
### Topic: University Life in the UK

**Host:** Today we're speaking with Emma, an exchange student from London. Emma, how is studying in Britain different from Kazakhstan?

**Emma:** Well, there are several differences. First, the teaching style is quite independent. Professors give lectures, but students are expected to do a lot of research on their own. Self-study is very important.

**Host:** What about student life outside classes?

**Emma:** University life in Britain is very active. There are hundreds of clubs and societies—for sports, music, drama, debate, and many other interests. Students often live in dormitories during their first year, which helps them make friends quickly.

**Host:** How do British universities assess students?

**Emma:** It varies by subject, but typically there are essays, presentations, and exams. Continuous assessment is common, meaning your grade comes from work throughout the semester, not just one final exam.

**Host:** Any advice for Kazakh students planning to study in the UK?

**Emma:** Improve your English as much as possible before you go. Also, be prepared to be independent. Don't be shy to ask for help when you need it—professors and staff are usually very supportive.

## TEXT 8 - B2 Level (Monologue) - ~200 words
### Topic: The Impact of Technology on Society

Technology has transformed virtually every aspect of modern life, bringing both remarkable benefits and significant challenges.

On the positive side, technology has revolutionized communication. Video calls allow families separated by thousands of kilometers to stay connected. Social media enables people to share experiences and ideas instantly across the globe. In medicine, advanced imaging technology and robotic surgery have saved countless lives.

However, technology also raises concerns. Privacy issues have become increasingly prominent as companies collect vast amounts of personal data. The rise of artificial intelligence threatens to automate many jobs, potentially leaving millions unemployed. Social media, while connecting people, has also been linked to increased rates of anxiety and depression, particularly among young people.

Perhaps the most significant challenge is finding balance. Technology should enhance our lives, not dominate them. Experts recommend setting boundaries—for instance, not using phones during meals or before bedtime.

Looking ahead, the pace of technological change shows no signs of slowing. It is essential that we guide this development thoughtfully, ensuring that innovations serve humanity's best interests rather than creating new problems.

## TEXT 9 - B2 Level (Dialogue) - ~210 words
### Topic: Scientific Discoveries and Research

**Journalist:** Dr. Nazarbayeva, your research on renewable energy has received international attention. Can you explain your recent breakthrough?

**Dr. Nazarbayeva:** We've developed a new type of solar panel that is significantly more efficient than current models. Traditional panels convert about twenty percent of sunlight into electricity. Our panels achieve nearly forty percent efficiency.

**Journalist:** How did you accomplish this?

**Dr. Nazarbayeva:** We used a combination of materials and a multi-layered structure. The key innovation was incorporating special nanoparticles that capture a broader spectrum of light. It took our team five years of research to reach this point.

**Journalist:** What are the practical implications?

**Dr. Nazarbayeva:** This could accelerate the transition to clean energy. More efficient panels mean fewer are needed to generate the same amount of electricity, reducing costs and space requirements. Eventually, this technology could make solar power competitive with fossil fuels in almost every market.

**Journalist:** What obstacles remain before commercialization?

**Dr. Nazarbayeva:** Manufacturing costs must come down. Currently, production is expensive because the materials are rare. We're working with industry partners to find more economical solutions. I'm optimistic that within five to seven years, these panels will be widely available.

## TEXT 10 - B2 Level (Interview) - ~220 words
### Topic: Traditions and Culture of English-Speaking Countries

**Presenter:** Welcome to World Cultures Today. Our topic is traditions in English-speaking countries. Professor Williams, what cultural traditions do Americans hold most dear?

**Professor Williams:** Americans celebrate several significant holidays. Thanksgiving, held in November, brings families together for a feast centered on turkey and gratitude. Independence Day on July Fourth features fireworks, parades, and celebrations of patriotism.

**Presenter:** How do British traditions differ?

**Professor Williams:** Britain has a deep royal heritage that influences many traditions. Events like the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace attract tourists worldwide. Afternoon tea, while less common today, remains a symbol of British culture. Christmas is celebrated with unique customs like the Boxing Day holiday and Christmas crackers.

**Presenter:** What about Australia and New Zealand?

**Professor Williams:** These countries have developed their own distinct traditions. Australia Day celebrates the nation's founding, while New Zealand's Waitangi Day honors the treaty between British colonizers and Maori people. Both countries share a love of outdoor activities—barbecues, beach celebrations, and rugby are central to their cultural identity.

**Presenter:** How do these nations maintain traditions in our globalized world?

**Professor Williams:** Many communities actively preserve their heritage through festivals, museums, and schools. However, traditions naturally evolve over time, absorbing influences from immigrant communities while maintaining core values.
